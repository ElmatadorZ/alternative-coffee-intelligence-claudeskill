## ☕ ARABICA VARIETIES

### TYPICA LINEAGE

**Typica (Original)**
- **Genetics:** Foundation variety, brought from Ethiopia via Yemen
- **Characteristics:**
  - Tall plant (up to 3-4m)
  - Low yield (1,000-1,500 kg/hectare)
  - Excellent cup quality (complex, clean, sweet)
  - Low disease resistance (susceptible to rust, CBD)
  - Long, narrow beans
- **Flavor profile:** Clean, sweet, balanced acidity, complex
- **Best altitudes:** 1,000-2,000 masl
- **Grown:** Latin America, India, Indonesia (historic)
- **Notable descendants:** Maragogype, Kent, Java, Blue Mountain

---

**Maragogype**
- **Genetics:** Natural Typica mutation (Bahia, Brazil, 1870)
- **Characteristics:**
  - GIANT beans (40-50% larger than Typica)
  - Very low yield
  - Large leaves, large cherries
  - Moderate disease resistance
- **Flavor profile:** Mild, low acidity, heavy body, less complexity
- **Best altitude:** 600-1,200 masl
- **Grown:** Guatemala, Mexico, Nicaragua
- **Roast approach:** Gentle heat, beans crack easier due to size

---

**SL28 & SL34**
- **Genetics:** Selected by Scott Labs (Kenya, 1930s) from Bourbon/Typica lines
- **SL28:**
  - Drought-tolerant (deep roots)
  - High cup quality
  - Moderate yield
  - Bronze leaf tips
- **SL34:**
  - Prefers higher rainfall
  - Heavier body than SL28
  - Green leaf tips
- **Flavor profile:** 
  - SL28: Intense acidity (phosphoric, citric), black currant, complex
  - SL34: Similar but slightly heavier body
- **Best altitude:** 1,400-2,100 masl
- **Grown:** Kenya (iconic), some Latin America transplants
- **Processing:** Typically washed (Kenyan double-fermentation)

---

**Geisha/Gesha**
- **Genetics:** Ethiopian landrace (Gesha village), transplanted to Panama
- **Characteristics:**
  - Tall, open branching
  - Very low yield (but high price compensates)
  - Highly susceptible to rust
  - Long, narrow beans
  - Requires high altitude
- **Flavor profile:** FLORAL (jasmine, bergamot), tea-like, delicate, complex, tropical fruit
- **Best altitude:** 1,500-2,000 masl
- **Grown:** Panama (most famous), Colombia, Costa Rica, Ethiopia (origin)
- **Price:** $50-1,000+/kg green (auction prices insane)
- **Roast approach:** Light, preserve florals, DTR ~20%
- **Brew: 93-95°C, 1:16-17, highlight floral volatiles

---

### BOURBON LINEAGE

**Bourbon (Red, Yellow, Pink)**
- **Genetics:** Natural Typica mutation (Réunion island, formerly Bourbon)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Shorter plant than Typica (~2.5-3m)
  - 20-30% higher yield than Typica
  - Excellent cup quality (sweet, balanced)
  - Low disease resistance
  - Round beans (vs Typica's narrow)
- **Red Bourbon:**
  - Original, most common
  - Balanced, sweet, fruity
- **Yellow Bourbon:**
  - Natural mutation (Brazil)
  - Sweeter, less acidity, creamy
- **Pink Bourbon:**
  - Rare mutation
  - Complex, fruity, unique
- **Flavor profile:** Sweet, fruit-forward, balanced acidity, creamy body
- **Best altitude:** 1,000-2,000 masl
- **Grown:** Latin America (especially Brazil, El Salvador, Rwanda)

---

**Caturra**
- **Genetics:** Natural Bourbon dwarf mutation (Brazil, 1937)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Short plant (2-2.5m) → easier harvest
  - Higher yield than Bourbon
  - Shorter internodes (more compact)
  - Good cup quality
  - Low disease resistance
- **Flavor profile:** Bright acidity, lighter body than Bourbon, clean
- **Best altitude:** 1,200-1,800 masl (needs altitude for quality)
- **Grown:** Colombia, Central America
- **Note:** Parent of many modern varieties (Catuai, Catimor crosses)

---

**Catuai (Red & Yellow)**
- **Genetics:** Caturra x Mundo Novo hybrid (Brazil, 1949)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Dwarf (2m)
  - High yield
  - Cherries hold tight (wind-resistant)
  - Good cup quality (not exceptional)
  - Low disease resistance
- **Flavor profile:** 
  - Red: Balanced, sweet, medium body
  - Yellow: Sweeter, lower acidity
- **Best altitude:** 800-1,500 masl
- **Grown:** Brazil, Central America
- **Use case:** Commercial production, reliable yield

---

**Pacamara**
- **Genetics:** Pacas (Bourbon dwarf) x Maragogype (El Salvador, 1958)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Large beans (from Maragogype parent)
  - Better yield than Maragogype
  - Medium plant height
  - Moderate disease resistance
- **Flavor profile:** Complex, floral, fruity, heavy body, chocolate notes
- **Best altitude:** 1,200-1,600 masl
- **Grown:** El Salvador, Guatemala, Nicaragua
- **Roast approach:** Can handle medium roast, complexity shines

---

### ETHIOPIAN HEIRLOOMS

**Ethiopian Landrace Varieties**
- **Genetics:** Wild, genetically diverse, NOT single varieties
- **Characteristics:**
  - Umbrella term for THOUSANDS of wild types
  - Each region has distinct genetic profiles
  - High genetic diversity = flavor diversity
  - Generally good disease resistance (co-evolved with pathogens)
- **Regions:**
  - **Yirgacheffe:** Floral, tea-like, bright
  - **Sidamo:** Fruity, wine-like, complex
  - **Harrar:** Blueberry, wine-like, natural process
  - **Limu:** Balanced, sweet, floral
- **Flavor range:** Wildly variable — jasmine to blueberry to wine
- **Processing:** Washed (clean, floral) or Natural (fruit bomb)
- **Roast approach:** Light to preserve origin character
- **Note:** Genetic goldmine, many modern varieties trace back

## 🔬 HYBRID & F1 VARIETIES

**Catimor**
- **Genetics:** Caturra x Híbrido de Timor (Timor Hybrid has Robusta genes)
- **Purpose:** Rust resistance (from Robusta parent)
- **Characteristics:**
  - High disease resistance (Coffee Leaf Rust)
  - High yield
  - Dwarf plant
  - **Cup quality: INFERIOR** (Robusta genetics compromise flavor)
- **Flavor:** Woody, earthy, lacks complexity, harsh
- **Use case:** Commercial volume production where rust is severe
- **Alternative Slowbar take:** Avoid unless rust crisis. Flavor sacrifice not worth it.

---

**Castillo**
- **Genetics:** Colombian research, Catimor derivative + quality backcrossing
- **Purpose:** Rust resistance with better cup quality than Catimor
- **Characteristics:**
  - High rust resistance
  - High yield
  - Medium plant height
  - BETTER cup than Catimor (but still not elite)
- **Flavor:** Clean, balanced, but lacks complexity of pure Arabica
- **Use case:** Colombia (widely planted post-rust epidemic)
- **Alternative Slowbar take:** Acceptable for blends, not specialty single-origin

---

**F1 Hybrids (Centroamericano, Starmaya, etc.)**
- **Genetics:** First-generation crosses (e.g., Ethiopian varieties x Latin American)
- **Purpose:** Heterosis (hybrid vigor) → yield + quality + disease resistance
- **Characteristics:**
  - 30-50% higher yield than parents
  - Good disease resistance
  - Excellent cup quality (combines parents' traits)
  - **Catch:** Seeds don't breed true (must propagate via grafting/tissue culture)
- **Flavor:** Complex, combines parent characteristics
- **Cost:** HIGH (tissue culture propagation expensive)
- **Use case:** Future of specialty (quality + yield + resilience)
- **Alternative Slowbar take:** Exciting. Addresses farmer economics without sacrificing cup.

## 💪 ROBUSTA (Coffea canephora)

**Robusta**
- **Genetics:** Different species from Arabica, native to Central/West Africa
- **Characteristics:**
  - Lower altitude (0-800 masl)
  - Higher yield than Arabica
  - Higher caffeine (~2.7% vs Arabica's 1.5%)
  - Higher disease resistance
  - Heat-tolerant
  - **Lower cup quality** (harsh, rubbery, grain-like)
- **Flavor:** Bitter, astringent, woody, peanut-like, low acidity
- **Use case:** 
  - Instant coffee
  - Italian espresso blends (crema boost)
  - Commodity market
- **Best quality Robusta:** India (Kaapi Royale), Vietnam (carefully processed)
- **Alternative Slowbar take:** 
  - Most Robusta = avoid
  - Fine Robusta (rare) = acceptable in espresso blends for body/crema
  - Never as single-origin unless truly exceptional

## 🎯 VARIETY RECOMMENDATIONS BY GOAL

### GOAL: Maximum Cup Quality
**Prioritize:**
1. Geisha (if altitude + budget allows)
2. SL28/34 (Kenyan coffees)
3. Ethiopian heirlooms (Yirgacheffe, etc.)
4. Typica (if rust can be managed)
5. Bourbon (especially Yellow for sweetness)

---

### GOAL: Balance Quality + Yield
**Prioritize:**
1. F1 Hybrids (best of both worlds, high initial cost)
2. Caturra (reliable, needs altitude)
3. Catuai (decent quality, good yield)
4. Pacamara (large beans, unique)

---

### GOAL: Rust Resistance (Disease-Prone Areas)
**Prioritize:**
1. F1 Hybrids (quality + resistance)
2. Castillo (acceptable cup, high resistance)
3. Catimor (only if budget/rust crisis, flavor sacrifice)

---

### GOAL: Low Altitude / Heat Tolerance
**Prioritize:**
1. Robusta (if quality expectations low)
2. Catimor (compromise solution)
3. Brazilian varieties (Mundo Novo, adapted)

## 🔬 GENETIC DEEP DIVE: Why Variety Matters

### Bean Density
- Higher altitude + slower maturation → denser bean
- Density → harder bean → more complex development in roasting
- **Geisha/SL28/Ethiopian heirlooms at high altitude:** Extremely dense
- **Catimor at low altitude:** Low density, less complex

### Chemical Composition
- **Sugars (9-12%):** Sweetness potential, Maillard reactions
  - Bourbon lineage: Higher sugar
  - Typica lineage: Balanced
- **Acids:** Brightness
  - SL28: High phosphoric acid (Kenyan brightness)
  - Ethiopian: Malic, citric (fruity, floral)
  - Robusta: Low acidity (flat)
- **Caffeine:**
  - Arabica: 1.2-1.5%
  - Robusta: 2.2-2.7%
  - Higher caffeine = more bitterness
- **Chlorogenic Acids (7-10%):**
  - Break down during roasting → quinic acid (bitter if over-roasted)
  - Robusta has more CGA → more bitterness potential

### Bean Morphology
- **Size:** Maragogype (huge) cracks easier, roasts faster
- **Shape:** Typica (long) vs Bourbon (round) affects heat distribution
- **Density:** Higher density needs more heat energy, longer roast

## 🌡️ ROASTING ADJUSTMENTS BY VARIETY

### High-Density Varieties (Geisha, SL28, Ethiopian heirlooms)
- **Approach:** More heat, longer development
- **First Crack:** Later (198-205°C)
- **Development:** 20-25% (preserve complexity)
- **Drop temp:** 202-208°C
- **Risk:** Under-development (grassy, vegetal)

### Low-Density Varieties (Catimor, low-altitude coffees)
- **Approach:** Less aggressive heat
- **First Crack:** Earlier (192-198°C)
- **Development:** 15-20% (avoid baking)
- **Drop temp:** 196-202°C
- **Risk:** Baking (flat, dull)

### Large Beans (Maragogype, Pacamara)
- **Approach:** Lower heat, slower
- **Risk:** Tipping (scorching) due to size
- **Strategy:** Lower charge temp, gentler RoR

### Delicate Varieties (Geisha)
- **Approach:** Light roast, preserve florals
- **Development:** 18-22%
- **Drop temp:** 200-205°C
- **Goal:** Express terroir, not roast character

## 🎓 ALTERNATIVE SLOWBAR TAKE

**Variety matters, but it's not everything:**

1. **Terroir > Variety** (sometimes)
   - Great terroir + Caturra > Bad terroir + Geisha

2. **Process > Variety** (sometimes)
   - Same variety, washed vs natural = different coffees

3. **Variety = Potential, not guarantee**
   - Geisha potential is high, but bad farming/processing = waste

4. **Farmer economics matter**
   - High-yield varieties (even if lower quality) can be ethical choice
   - F1 hybrids solve this (quality + yield)

5. **Variety hype is real**
   - Geisha prices are often inflated beyond quality
   - Some auctions are speculation, not value

6. **Best approach:**
   - Know variety characteristics
   - Adjust roasting accordingly
   - But judge cup, not name
