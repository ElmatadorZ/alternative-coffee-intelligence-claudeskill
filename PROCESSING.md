## 🎯 PROCESSING OVERVIEW

**What is processing?**
The method of removing coffee bean from cherry and preparing it for export.

**Why it matters:**
- 40-60% of final cup character comes from processing
- Same coffee, different process = different flavor profiles
- Processing = flavor development tool (if done right) or defect generator (if done wrong)

---

## 📊 PROCESSING METHOD COMPARISON

| Method | Fermentation | Drying Time | Flavor Impact | Risk Level | Water Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Washed** | Yes (24-48h) | 10-14 days | Clean, bright | Medium | High |
| **Natural** | Yes (in cherry) | 20-30 days | Fruit-forward | High | Zero |
| **Honey** | Partial | 12-18 days | Balanced | Medium | Low |
| **Anaerobic** | Yes (sealed) | Varies | Intense, complex | Very High | Varies |
| **Wet-Hulled** | Short | 2-3 days | Earthy, heavy | Medium | Medium |

## 💧 METHOD 1: WASHED (WET PROCESS)

### Process Flow:
```
Cherry harvest
    ↓
Float (remove floaters = defects)
    ↓
Depulp (remove skin + most fruit)
    ↓
Ferment (24-72h, break down mucilage)
    ↓
Wash (remove fermented mucilage)
    ↓
Dry (10-14 days to 10-12% moisture)
    ↓
Rest (1-2 months in parchment)
    ↓
Mill (remove parchment)
    ↓
Export
```

### Fermentation Phase:
**What's happening:**
- Mucilage (pectin-rich layer) surrounds parchment
- Microbes (bacteria, yeast) consume sugars
- Enzymes break down pectin structure
- Byproducts: acids, alcohols, esters

**Key variables:**
1. **Time:** 24-72 hours (varies by temperature, altitude)
2. **Temperature:** 18-22°C ideal (tropical = faster, highland = slower)
3. **pH:** Starts ~5.5, drops to ~4.0-4.5 (acidity)
4. **Microbiology:** 
   - **Bacteria:** Lactic acid bacteria (LAB), acetic acid bacteria
   - **Yeast:** Saccharomyces, Candida, Pichia
5. **Oxygen:** Aerobic initially, becomes anaerobic as O2 depleted

**Monitoring:**
- **Feel test:** Rub beans, if parchment feels rough (not slimy) → done
- **pH test:** pH ~4.0-4.5 = complete
- **Visual:** Water runs clear, no mucilage visible
- **Time:** 24-48h in highlands, 12-24h in lowlands

**Over-fermentation risks:**
- Vinegar notes (acetic acid from over-fermentation)
- Onion/phenolic defects (bacterial contamination)
- Fermented defects (butyric acid)

**Under-fermentation risks:**
- Mucilage remains → uneven drying → mold risk
- Less acidity development

### Washing Phase:
**Purpose:** Remove fermented mucilage completely
**Method:** 
- Channels with running water
- Mechanical agitation
- Multiple rinses until water runs clear

**Kenyan Double-Fermentation:**
- Ferment 1: 24h
- Wash
- Ferment 2: 24h (with fresh water)
- Wash
- **Result:** Ultra-clean, intense acidity (phosphoric acid character)

### Drying Phase:
**Target:** 10-12% moisture content
**Methods:**
1. **Patio drying:** Thin layer (2-3 cm) on raised beds/patios
2. **African beds:** Raised mesh beds (better airflow)
3. **Mechanical dryer:** Guardiola dryer (faster, controlled)

**Key variables:**
- **Temperature:** Max 40-45°C (higher = case hardening, cracks)
- **Airflow:** Critical for even drying, prevent mold
- **Thickness:** Thinner layer = faster, more even
- **Turning:** Every 30-60 min (prevent fermentation pockets)
- **Cover:** At night (prevent moisture reabsorption)

**Drying physics:**
- Week 1: Fast moisture loss (40-50% → 20%)
- Week 2: Slower (20% → 10-12%)
- **Critical:** Last few percentage points = easy to over-dry or under-dry

**Monitoring:**
- **Moisture meter:** Target 10-12%, measure multiple samples
- **Sound test:** Shake beans, should rattle (dry parchment)
- **Visual:** Light brown parchment, no green/dark spots

### Flavor Profile:
**Characteristics:**
- **Clean cup:** Minimal processing influence
- **Bright acidity:** Malic, citric, phosphoric (depending on origin)
- **Clarity:** Origin character shines
- **Light-medium body:** Less body than natural
- **Complexity:** High (if well-processed)

**Best for:**
- High-altitude coffees (preserve terroir)
- Varieties with good acidity (SL28, Ethiopian, Geisha)
- Markets valuing clarity and brightness

**Alternative Slowbar approach:**
- Washed = default for most origins
- Expresses terroir best
- Less risky than natural (if farm has water access)

## ☀️ METHOD 2: NATURAL (DRY PROCESS)

### Process Flow:
```
Cherry harvest (only ripe cherries!)
    ↓
Float (remove defects)
    ↓
Dry WHOLE cherry (20-30 days)
    ↓
Rest (1-2 months)
    ↓
Mill (remove dried cherry skin + parchment)
    ↓
Export
```

### Drying Phase (Critical):
**Challenge:** Entire cherry must dry evenly without fermenting/molding

**Key variables:**
1. **Cherry selection:** ONLY ripe cherries (unripes = astringency)
2. **Layer thickness:** Very thin (1-2 cherries deep)
3. **Turning frequency:** Every 30-45 min for first week
4. **Drying surface:** Raised beds (airflow underneath)
5. **Climate:** Dry season essential (rain = disaster)

**Fermentation in natural:**
- Happens INSIDE cherry
- Anaerobic fermentation (limited oxygen)
- Sugars + pulp → alcohols, esters, organic acids
- **Result:** Fruity, wine-like, complex (when done right)

**Phases:**
- Days 1-7: Fast moisture loss, active fermentation, sweet/boozy smell
- Days 8-15: Slower drying, fermentation slows, fruity aroma
- Days 16-30: Final drying, cherry becomes brittle

**Risks:**
- **Mold:** If drying too slow or humid
- **Over-fermentation:** Vinegar, alcohol, fermented defects
- **Uneven drying:** Some cherries dry faster → inconsistency
- **Insect damage:** Longer exposure time
- **Rain:** Ruins batch (rehydration → fermentation)

**Quality control:**
- Sort out defects BEFORE drying (or they infect batch)
- Turn constantly (labor-intensive)
- Cover at night (dew = moisture)
- Smell test daily (no off-odors)

### Flavor Profile:
**Characteristics:**
- **Fruit-forward:** Berry, tropical fruit, wine-like
- **Heavy body:** Full, syrupy
- **Lower acidity:** Compared to washed (acids consumed in fermentation)
- **Complexity:** High (but different from washed)
- **Sweetness:** High (sugars absorbed into bean)

**Best for:**
- Ethiopian coffees (traditional method)
- Origins with distinct fruit character
- Dry climates (low humidity, no rain during harvest)
- Markets liking fruit-forward profiles

**Not ideal for:**
- Wet climates (mold risk too high)
- Farms lacking labor (turning is intensive)
- Markets preferring clean cups

**Alternative Slowbar take:**
- Natural = high risk, high reward
- When done well = exceptional
- When done poorly = defects galore
- **Honesty check:** Is "natural process" hype justified by quality? Or marketing?

## 🍯 METHOD 3: HONEY (PULPED NATURAL)

### Process Flow:
```
Cherry harvest
    ↓
Depulp (remove skin, KEEP mucilage)
    ↓
Dry with mucilage (12-18 days)
    ↓
Rest
    ↓
Mill
    ↓
Export
```

### Honey Levels (mucilage retained):
| Type | Mucilage % | Drying Time | Flavor |
|---|---|---|---|
| **White Honey** | 10-25% | 8-10 days | Close to washed |
| **Yellow Honey** | 25-50% | 10-12 days | Balanced |
| **Red Honey** | 50-75% | 12-15 days | Fruity, sweet |
| **Black Honey** | 75-100% | 15-20 days | Almost like natural |

### Characteristics:
- **Balance:** Between washed (clean) and natural (fruit)
- **Body:** Medium-heavy (more than washed, less than natural)
- **Sweetness:** High (sugar from mucilage absorbed)
- **Acidity:** Moderate (less than washed, more than natural)

### Key Variables:
- **Mucilage amount:** More mucilage = longer drying, more fruit character
- **Drying method:** Raised beds essential (sticky, needs airflow)
- **Turning:** Less frequent than natural (mucilage is sticky)
- **Climate:** Needs dry weather (like natural)

### Risks:
- **Sticking:** Beans stick together if too much mucilage
- **Mold:** Sweet mucilage attracts microbes
- **Inconsistency:** Hard to control mucilage % precisely

### Alternative Slowbar take:
- Honey = marketing term that's now meaningless
- "Red honey", "black honey" = often arbitrary
- **What matters:** How much mucilage, how long dried
- Good honey process = balanced, sweet, but can be gimmicky
- Prefer washed or natural for clarity of intent

## 🧪 METHOD 4: ANAEROBIC FERMENTATION

### Process Flow:
```
Cherry harvest
    ↓
Depulp (or keep whole for anaerobic natural)
    ↓
Place in SEALED TANK (no oxygen)
    ↓
Ferment (24-200+ hours, monitored)
    ↓
Remove, wash (if washed variant)
    ↓
Dry
    ↓
Export
```

### Science:
**Why sealed?**
- Eliminates oxygen
- Forces anaerobic fermentation
- Different microbes dominate (anaerobes)
- Different chemical pathways
- **Result:** More esters, alcohols, fruity compounds

**Key variables:**
1. **Time:** 24-200+ hours (wild variation)
2. **Temperature:** Controlled (cool = slower, warmer = faster)
3. **pH:** Monitored (drops as acids form)
4. **CO2 buildup:** Must release pressure (valves)
5. **Starter culture:** Some use inoculated yeast (like wine)

**Chemical reactions:**
- Glycolysis: Sugars → pyruvate → ethanol + CO2
- Ester formation: Alcohols + acids → fruity esters
- LAB fermentation: Lactic acid, lactic acid esters

**Monitoring:**
- **pH meter:** Track acidity (shouldn't drop too low)
- **Temperature probe:** Keep stable
- **Pressure gauge:** Release CO2 periodically
- **Time:** Recipe-dependent (but monitor, don't just timer)
- **Sensory:** Sample beans, smell, taste

### Flavor Profile:
**Characteristics:**
- **Intense fruit:** Strawberry, tropical fruit, wine
- **Complex:** Layers of flavor
- **Boozy/fermented:** (can be positive or defect)
- **Experimental:** Wide variation depending on protocol

**Risks:**
- **Very high:** Small changes = big flavor shifts
- **Over-fermentation:** Vinegar, off-flavors
- **Inconsistency:** Hard to replicate batch-to-batch
- **Defects:** Alcohol, fermented, onion

**Cost:**
- High (equipment, monitoring, expertise)

**Alternative Slowbar take:**
- Anaerobic = exciting when done well
- But often gimmicky, over-fermented
- **Honest question:** Does this improve coffee, or just make it weird?
- Best use: Enhance already-great coffee, not mask poor quality
- Prefer traditional methods unless producer truly understands science

## 🌏 METHOD 5: WET-HULLED (GILING BASAH) — Indonesia

### Process Flow:
```
Cherry harvest
    ↓
Depulp
    ↓
Ferment (short, 12-24h)
    ↓
Wash
    ↓
Dry (2-3 days, to ~30-40% moisture, still semi-wet!)
    ↓
HULL (remove parchment while still wet)
    ↓
Dry naked bean (to 10-12%)
    ↓
Export
```

### Why this method?
**Context:** Indonesia = high humidity, frequent rain
**Problem:** Traditional drying takes too long, mold risk high
**Solution:** Remove parchment early, dry naked bean faster

### Characteristics:
- Naked bean dries faster (no parchment barrier)
- **But:** Bean is soft, absorbs flavors, oxidizes

### Flavor Profile:
**Characteristics:**
- **Earthy:** Soil, tobacco, cedar, herbal
- **Heavy body:** Thick, syrupy
- **Low acidity:** Muted, flat
- **Unique:** Love it or hate it

**Why this profile?**
- Early hulling → bean exposed to environment
- Absorbs earthy/herbal notes
- Oxidation changes chemistry

**Best for:**
- Sumatra, Sulawesi, Java (traditional)
- Espresso blends (body, low acidity)
- Markets liking earthy profiles

**Alternative Slowbar take:**
- Wet-hulled = necessary compromise for Indonesia climate
- Not a "quality" method, but cultural tradition
- **Honesty:** Don't romanticize. It's earthy because of process, not terroir.
- Good wet-hulled = clean version of this (no defects)
- Poor wet-hulled = moldy, dirty

## 📊 PROCESS SELECTION DECISION TREE

### Question 1: What's the climate?
- **Dry season, low humidity** → Natural or Honey possible
- **High humidity, frequent rain** → Washed (or wet-hulled if Indonesia)

### Question 2: What's the water availability?
- **Plentiful clean water** → Washed
- **Limited water** → Natural or Honey

### Question 3: What's the labor availability?
- **High labor** → Natural (intensive turning) or Washed (fermentation management)
- **Low labor** → Honey (less turning) or Mechanical drying

### Question 4: What's the target market?
- **Specialty, quality-focused** → Washed (clarity) or Experimental Anaerobic
- **Fruit-forward preference** → Natural or Honey
- **Commercial volume** → Washed (most reliable) or Wet-hulled

### Question 5: What's the risk tolerance?
- **Low risk** → Washed (most forgiving)
- **Medium risk** → Honey
- **High risk** → Natural, Anaerobic

## 🧬 FERMENTATION SCIENCE DEEP DIVE

### Microbiology:
**Key organisms:**
1. **Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB):**
   - Lactobacillus, Leuconostoc
   - Produce lactic acid (yogurt-like tang)
   - Anaerobic or facultative

2. **Acetic Acid Bacteria (AAB):**
   - Acetobacter
   - Produce acetic acid (vinegar)
   - Aerobic (need oxygen)
   - **Overactive = defect**

3. **Yeast:**
   - Saccharomyces (like beer/wine)
   - Candida, Pichia (wild yeasts)
   - Produce ethanol, esters (fruity)
   - Anaerobic fermentation

4. **Mold:**
   - Aspergillus, Penicillium
   - **Always bad** (mycotoxins, off-flavors)

### Chemical Pathways:

**Glycolysis (sugar breakdown):**
```
Glucose → Pyruvate → (anaerobic) → Ethanol + CO2
                   → (aerobic) → Citric acid cycle
```

**Ester formation:**
```
Alcohol + Organic acid → Ester (fruity aroma)
Example: Ethanol + Acetic acid → Ethyl acetate (fruity, nail polish)
```

**Maillard precursors:**
- Amino acids + reducing sugars → flavor precursors
- Develop in roasting, but formed during fermentation

### Variables Control:

| Variable | Effect | Control Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Temperature** | Higher = faster fermentation | Shade, water cooling, timing |
| **Time** | Longer = more fermentation | Monitor pH, sensory |
| **pH** | Lower = more acidic, inhibits some microbes | Measure, adjust time |
| **Oxygen** | Aerobic vs anaerobic pathways | Seal tank (anaerobic) or open (aerobic) |
| **Inoculation** | Controlled vs wild fermentation | Add starter culture or let natural |

## 🎯 ALTERNATIVE SLOWBAR PROCESSING PHILOSOPHY

### 1. **Process should serve the coffee, not dominate it**
- Best processing = invisible (lets terroir shine)
- Over-processing = gimmick (masks poor quality)

### 2. **Traditional methods first**
- Washed, Natural, Honey = proven
- Experimental = interesting, but prove it improves coffee

### 3. **Honesty in flavor source**
- Fruity because of terroir? Or fermentation?
- Transparent about what process contributes

### 4. **Risk assessment**
- High-risk methods need expertise
- Don't push farmers into anaerobic if they can't monitor properly

### 5. **Water scarcity matters**
- Washed uses lots of water
- In water-scarce regions, Natural/Honey more ethical

### 6. **Consistency > experimentation**
- Repeatable quality > one-off crazy batch
- Experimental is fine, but master basics first

## 📋 PROCESSING DEFECTS & DIAGNOSIS

### Common Defects by Processing:

**Washed:**
- **Fermented:** Over-fermentation (acetic, onion, phenolic)
- **Stinker:** One over-fermented bean contaminates batch
- **Quaker:** Unripe bean that didn't ferment properly (pale, no flavor)

**Natural:**
- **Mold:** Slow drying, high humidity
- **Fermented:** Over-fermentation inside cherry
- **Uneven drying:** Some beans under-dried (mold risk)

**Honey:**
- **Sticky:** Beans stuck together, uneven drying
- **Fermented:** Mucilage over-fermented

**Anaerobic:**
- **Alcohol:** Too much ethanol production
- **Vinegar:** Acetic acid from aerobic contamination
- **Inconsistency:** Batch variation
